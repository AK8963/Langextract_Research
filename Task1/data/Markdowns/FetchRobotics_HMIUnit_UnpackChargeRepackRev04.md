## 1. OVERVIEW

The purpose of this work instruction is to provide the procedure for unpacking, charging, and repacking the HMI Shelf Robot.

## 2. INSPECTION PROCESS

- **1.** Move the robot to the staging area—Reference *Subinventory Transfer Rev1.0 work instructions*.
- **2.** Inspect the shipment for any possible mishandling: (a) Crate, (b) Shockwatch indicator, and (c) Tip 'N' Tell indicator.
  - a) The crate should not have any writing or drawing on it.

![](_page_0_Picture_8.jpeg)

b) Shockwatch indicator of mishandling

![](_page_0_Picture_10.jpeg)

![](_page_0_Picture_11.jpeg)

c) Tip 'N' Tell indicator of mishandling.

![](_page_0_Picture_13.jpeg)

![](_page_0_Picture_14.jpeg)

If the arrow point is blue, the shipment was mishandled.

*This document contains proprietary information which is the exclusive property of Zebra Technologies Corp.*

- ✓ If you find any issues or concerns of mishandling, please notify the Zebra Engineering and Quality department immediately.
- **3.** Robots must be charged to full at least once every six months per the battery manufacturer's specifications.
  - ✓ If a unit is not changed at four or five and half months, it must be charged at the 6-month mark regardless of the charge level.
- **4.** If the robot does not require charging, follow section 4 of this work instruction.

## 3. UNPACK PROCESS

- **1.** Move the freight crate to the charging area. Reference *Subinventory Transfer Rev1.0 work instruction*.
- **2.** Carefully remove the latches on the top and sides.
- **3.** Remove the front cover.

![](_page_1_Picture_8.jpeg)

- **4.** Unsecure the strap across the ramp.
  - ✓ The strap could be yellow or black; there is no difference.

![](_page_1_Picture_11.jpeg)

- **5.** Fold down the ramp from the crate.
  - ✓ Do not step on the ramp.

✓ The ramp should not have any markings.

![](_page_2_Picture_1.jpeg)

![](_page_2_Picture_2.jpeg)

- **6.** Slide out the top foam protection and the Push/Slide wood-backed foam insert from the slots on the crate's sides.
  - ✓ If the foam detaches from each other, use the heat gun to warm up the glue and reattach the foam.

![](_page_2_Picture_5.jpeg)

![](_page_2_Picture_6.jpeg)

![](_page_2_Picture_7.jpeg)

![](_page_2_Picture_8.jpeg)

- **7.** Remove the clear tape from the protective bag. If the bag is heat-sealed use scissors to cut the bag open.
  - ✓ If using scissors to cut the bag, try to cut it as smoothly as possible and as close to the sealing seam as possible to reuse the bag.

![](_page_2_Picture_11.jpeg)

**8.** Keep the desiccant bag inside the protective bag in the crate.

![](_page_3_Picture_1.jpeg)

- **9.** Use tape or plastic clamps to secure the plastic bag to the ramp, top, and sides of the wood panels so the bag is not in your way of unpacking or repacking the robot.
- **10.** Wea latex gloves and roll the HMI unit from the bag and the crate.
- **11.** Ensure you are rolling the HMI unit from the shelves as shown below; do not touch the screen, sensor, or camera.

![](_page_3_Picture_5.jpeg)

✓The plastic bag could be black or translucent blue (most common).

![](_page_3_Picture_7.jpeg)

- **12.** Inspect the robot for any visible damage.
- **13.** Keep all foam, protective bag, and desiccant bags inside the crate and close the crate with the door with at least 2-3 latches.
- **14.** Reference *Manual Charging Process Rev03* to manually charge the robot battery.

*This document contains proprietary information which is the exclusive property of Zebra Technologies Corp.*

- **15.** Ensure the Robot is cleaned.
- **16.** Robot Cleaning Guidelines:
  - a). Microfiber Lint-free towel/cloth will be used on a Class A area of the Robot (such as LCD screen & control panel).
  - b). IPA with shop towels or Kimwipes can be used if you find scuff marks on sheet metal.
  - c). Kimwipes can be used if you find fingerprints/marks on the screen, camera, and laser component.
- **17.** Ensure the breaker switch is on the OFF position before repacking.

![](_page_4_Picture_6.jpeg)

Breaker Switch

## 4. REPACK PROCESS

- **1.** Open the Freight Crate using the latches on the sides top and remove the front cover.
- **2.** Fold out the ramp completely to allow the robot to be rolled into the crate.

![](_page_4_Picture_11.jpeg)

![](_page_4_Picture_12.jpeg)

- **3.** Unfold the protective bag and remove the foam and desiccant bag for repacking.
- **4.** Tape or clamp the opening of the plastic bag to the ramp, top, and sides of the crate for easy loading of the robot into the crate.

![](_page_4_Picture_15.jpeg)

*This document contains proprietary information which is the exclusive property of Zebra Technologies Corp.*

- **5.** Roll the HMI unit through the ramp into the crate.
  - ✓ If you can easily roll the Robot into the crate, that's due to the Run-Stop button being released. If it is in released status, leave it as is. There is no need to engage.

![](_page_5_Picture_2.jpeg)

Run-Stop Button

![](_page_5_Picture_4.jpeg)

- ✓ Ensure the back of the robot where the breaker is located faces the back of the crate, and the front of the robot faces outward; the front has the camera and laser.
- **6.** Reuse the original desiccant bags and place them as they were when unpacking the Robot.

![](_page_5_Picture_7.jpeg)

- **7.** Remove the tape used to secure the plastic bag to the crate's top, bottom, and sides.
  - ✓ Usually, 2-3 desiccant bags inside the protective bag and one outside the bag in the crate

**8.** Cover the HMI unit and fold the barrier bag.

![](_page_6_Picture_1.jpeg)

- **9.** Apply clear tape to the bag to close it.
  - ✓ Don't heat-sealed the bag.
- **10.** Slide the wood-backed foam insert into the slots on the sides of the crate and ensure the top foam insert is pressed firmly against the HMI screen to protect it.

![](_page_6_Picture_5.jpeg)

**11.** Fold up the ramp into the crate and latch it.

![](_page_6_Picture_7.jpeg)

- **12.** Align the top edge of the crate's front cover with the top lip of the crate and latch it into place.
- **13.** Align the bottom edge and clamp. You might need a rubber mallet to force the front cover into place.
- **14.** Latch the bottom and the sides of the front cover to the crate.

![](_page_7_Picture_3.jpeg)

- **15.** The robot must be monitored for recharging until it is shipped.
- **16.** Put a green cone on the crate, move it to the staging area, and ask an associate to put it away.
