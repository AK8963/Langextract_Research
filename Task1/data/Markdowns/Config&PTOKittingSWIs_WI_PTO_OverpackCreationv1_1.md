## **PTO – OVERPACK CREATION**

The PTO Overpacks can be created when individual kits need to be packed into an Overpack or onto a Pallet, with a new LPN. Again, the Oracle Packing Workbench is used for this process.

![](_page_0_Picture_5.jpeg)

Please ensure that you are logged into the Overpack Label printer located on the PTO Station you will be using. Before you start kitting, please ensure that the file is displayed in your task bar.

**Step 1:** Use responsibility 'ZTC Warehouse System Packer' And navigate to 'Packing Workbench' Enter the correct Organization and press 'OK'

**Step 2:** Scan the barcode for the Packing Station you are working on

![](_page_0_Figure_9.jpeg)

Then scan the Delivery number from the PTO Kit Sheet

By default, the Packing Process option is set to 'LPN Split'. However, to create the Overpacks we must select the 'LPN Pack' option instead or scan the barcode

![](_page_0_Figure_12.jpeg)

![](_page_0_Figure_13.jpeg)

Click on 'Find'

**Step 3:** The Packing Workbench will be displayed Some important features:

![](_page_0_Picture_16.jpeg)

- Packing Message: this message indicates the next step in the Overpack process
- Content Information: details of current scan
- LPN Content: This section shows the Kits already scanned into the Overpack
- Eligible Material: This section shows the items, LPNs (Kits) that can be used for the Overpack

![](_page_0_Figure_21.jpeg)

**Step 4:** To create an Overpack, scan each LPN for the Kits you want to pack into the Overpack or onto the Pallet. It is up to the team to determine how many kits can be packed into an overpack or placed on a pallet

*Note: The Packing Message, Content Information, LPN Contents and Eligible Material details will be updated after* 

*each scan.*

When the Overpack or Pallet is full, this must be validated and closed, and the Overpack/Pallet label must be generated and applied.

To do this, scan the barcode for 'Validate, Close, Label LPN' or 'Close Overpack LPN' on the PTO Station

This will validate and close the Overpack, and it will generate the Overpack label

![](_page_1_Figure_8.jpeg)

*Note: At this stage there is still a Parent-Child relationship between the new Overpack LPN (Parent) and the Kits (Children) inside*

![](_page_1_Figure_10.jpeg)

![](_page_1_Figure_11.jpeg)

**Step 5:** The final step in the creation of the Overpack is to merge the LPNs, to remove the Parent-Child link

 Using the 'Find' screen again (via the search light icon) now select option 'LPN Reconfigure'

Or scan the 'Go to Merge-Up' barcode:

![](_page_1_Figure_15.jpeg)

![](_page_1_Figure_16.jpeg)

To complete the merge-up, scan the Overpack Parent LPN and click on 'Merge-Up' or scan the 'Merge-Up LPN' barcode

![](_page_1_Figure_18.jpeg)

![](_page_1_Figure_19.jpeg)

Important: When the overpacking process is completed, it is important to ensure that all Kits/Overpack have been merged-up. When all merge-ups are completed, the 'Parent LPN' field will be blank.
