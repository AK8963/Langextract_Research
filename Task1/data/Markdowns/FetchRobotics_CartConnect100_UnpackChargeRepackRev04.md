#### 1. OVERVIEW

The purpose of this work instruction is to provide the procedure for unpacking, charging, and repacking the Cart Connect 100 Robot.

#### 2. INSPECTION PROCESS

- **1.** Move the robot to the staging area—Reference *Subinventory Transfer Rev1.0 work instructions*.
- **2.** Inspect the shipment for any possible mishandling: (a) crate, (b) Shockwatch indicator, and (c) Tip 'N' Tell indicator.
  - a) The crate should not be damaged or have any writing or drawing on the crate.

![](_page_0_Picture_8.jpeg)

b) Shockwatch indicator of mishandling

![](_page_0_Picture_10.jpeg)

![](_page_0_Picture_11.jpeg)

Red indicates the shipment was mishandled.

c) Tip 'N' Tell indicator of mishandling.

![](_page_0_Picture_14.jpeg)

![](_page_0_Picture_15.jpeg)

If the arrow point is blue, the shipment was mishandled.

- ✓ If you find any issues or concerns of mishandling, please notify the Zebra Engineering and Quality department immediately.
- **3.** Robots must be charged to full at least once every six months per the battery manufacturer's specifications.
  - ✓ If a unit is not changed at four or five and half months, it must be charged at the 6-month mark regardless of the charge level.
- **4.** If the robot does not require charging, follow section 4 of this work instruction.

#### 3. UNPACK PROCESS

- **1.** Move the freight crate to the charging area. Reference *Subinventory Transfer V1.0 work instruction*.
- **2.** Open it by using the latches on the top and sides.
- **3.** Remove the front cover.

![](_page_1_Picture_8.jpeg)

- **4.** Unsecure the strap across the ramp.
  - ✓ The strap could be yellow or black; there is no difference.

![](_page_1_Picture_11.jpeg)

- **5.** Fold down the ramp from the crate.
  - ✓ Do not step on the ramp.
  - ✓ The ramp should not have any markings.

![](_page_2_Picture_3.jpeg)

- **6.** Remove the Push/Slide wood-backed foam insert from the slots on the crate's sides.
  - ✓ If the foam detaches from each other, use the heat gun to warm up the glue and reattach the foam.

![](_page_2_Picture_6.jpeg)

- **7.** Remove the clear tape from the protective bag; if the bag is heat-sealed, use scissors to cut the bag open.
  - ✓ If using scissors to cut the bag, try to cut it as smoothly as possible and as close to the sealing seam as possible to reuse the bag.
- **8.** Always keep the desiccant bags inside the protective bag in the crate.

![](_page_2_Picture_10.jpeg)

**9.** Use tape or plastic clamps to secure the protective bag to the ramp, side, and top of the wood panels so the bag is not in your way of unpacking or repacking the robot.

![](_page_3_Picture_0.jpeg)

✓ The plastic bag could be black or translucent blue (most common).

![](_page_3_Picture_2.jpeg)

- **10.** Wear latex gloves and remove the Cart Connect from the crate. You can pull the robot from the blue sheet metal cover.
  - ✓ Do not touch the Camera & Laser & Cart Connect Pins.

![](_page_3_Picture_5.jpeg)

![](_page_3_Picture_7.jpeg)

- **11.** Inspect the robot for any visible damage.
- **12.** Keep all foam, protective bag, and desiccant bags inside the crate and close the crate with the door with at least 2-3 latches.
- **13.** Reference *Manual Charging Process Rev03* to charge the robot battery manually.
- **14.** Ensure the Robot is cleaned.
- **15.** Robot Cleaning Guidelines:
  - a). Microfiber Lint-free towel/cloth will be used on a Class A area of the Robot (such as LCD screen & control panel).
  - b). IPA with shop towels or Kimwipes can be used if you find scuff marks on sheet metal.
  - c). Kimwipes can be used if you find fingerprints/marks on the screen, camera, and laser component.
- **16.** Ensure the breaker switch is on the OFF position before repacking.

#### 4. REPACK PROCESS

**1.** Open the Freight Crate by using the latches on the sides and top and removing the front cover.

![](_page_4_Picture_2.jpeg)

- **2.** Fold out the ramp completely to allow the robot to be rolled into the crate.
- **3.** Unfold the protective bag, remove the foam, and desiccant bag for repacking.
- **4.** Tape or clamp the opening of the protective bag to the bottom of the ramp and side wood panels for easy loading of the robot into the crate.
- **5.** Engage the RunStop button on the robot and slowly roll the robot up the ramp into the crate.
  - ✓ Ensure the back of the robot where the breaker is located faces the back of the crate, and the front of the robot faces outward; the front has the laser.

![](_page_4_Picture_8.jpeg)

Run-Stop Button

![](_page_4_Picture_10.jpeg)

- **6.** Reuse the original desiccant bags and place them as they were when unpacking the Robot.
  - ✓ Usually, 2-3 desiccant bags are inside the protective bag, and one is outside the bag in the crate.

![](_page_4_Picture_13.jpeg)

**7.** Fold the barrier bag and cover the Robot completely with the bag.

![](_page_5_Picture_1.jpeg)

- **8.** Apply clear tape to the bag.
  - ✓ Don't heat-sealed the bag.
- **9.** Slide the wood-backed foam insert into the slots on the sides of the crate. Ensure the insert is pressed firmly against the front of the robot.

![](_page_5_Picture_5.jpeg)

**10.** Fold up the ramp into the crate.

![](_page_5_Picture_7.jpeg)

**11.** Secure the strap across the ramp.

![](_page_6_Picture_1.jpeg)

**12.** Align the top edge of the crate's front cover with the top lip of the crate and latch it into place.

![](_page_6_Picture_3.jpeg)

- **13.** Align the bottom edge and clamp. You might need a rubber mallet to force the front cover into place.
- **14.** Latch the front cover's top and sides to the crate.
- **15.** The robot must be monitored for recharging until it is shipped.
- **16.** Put a green cone on the crate, move it to the staging area, and ask an associate to put it away.
