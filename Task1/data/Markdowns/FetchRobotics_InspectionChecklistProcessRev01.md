## 1. OVERVIEW

The purpose of this work instruction is to provide the procedure for completing the Inspection Checklist and who to contact in the event the Robot was mishandled.

### 2. INBOUND

- **1.** Before releasing the driver ensure the bill of lading is marked accordingly if there were any signs of mishandling.
- **2.** Obtain a copy of the *Fetch Robot Inspection Checklist Rev01*.
- **3.** Write the date the Robot was received and the name of the receiver.
- **4.** Write the Part number and the Serial # on the Checklist.
- **5.** Enter your initials in the Accepted or Rejected columns when completing sections 1.1 1.3 of the Inspection Checklist.
- **6.** Inspect the Crate and the indicators.
  - ✓ The crate should be free of damage and hand-written elements.
  - ✓ The ShockWatch label should not display red.
  - ✓ The Tip 'N' Tell Indicators should not have any blue particles on the arrow tip.

![](_page_0_Picture_14.jpeg)

- **7.** If there are NO rejections, place Inspection Checklist Rev01 in a white pouch and affix it to the crate.
- **8.** Follow regular receiving and put away process.

#### **Indicators Mishandled**

- **9.** If the indicators show signs of mishandling, informed VAS, and QA, the Robot needs to be taken out and visually inspected. Reference *F100 & accessories - Cosmetic Surface Classification.*
  - ✓ Mark the Bill of Lading accordingly, the logistics team might need to file a claim against the carrier.
- **10.** If the Robot is damaged, take pictures of the damage and send an email to the logistics team **Eva Mireles** Logistics Analyst, and **Mike Alaimo** – Senior Mgr. Fetch Operations, **Shailesh Jaiswal** – Manufacturing Engineer, **Eloy Rodas** – Quality Engineer, **Christian Cox** – Test Engineer
- **11.** Move the Material to MRB1 holding area and await disposition.
- **12.** If the Robot is not damaged, remove the indicators that were activated with the mishandling.
  - ✓ Apply 2 new Tip 'N' Tell and remove the pin holder to activate them.
  - ✓ Ensure they are placed in perpendicular format, 1 on the side and 1 in the back.
  - ✓ Do not place them on the door where the hinges are located.
- **13.** Make a note in the comments section of the Inspection Checklist if the indicators were replaced.
- **14.** Move units to stock.

![](_page_0_Picture_28.jpeg)

#### **Missing Indicators**

- **15.** The Robot needs to be visually inspected if the Tip 'N' Tell and ShockWatch indicators are missing.
- **16.** If there is no damage, apply new indicators and remove the pin holders to activate them.

#### **Indicators on the Crate**

- **17.** The Robot needs to be visually inspected if the pin holders are still in place.
- **18.** Pull the pin holders to activate the indicators if there is NO damage. Make a note on the inspection checklist.
- **19.** If the pin holders are not in perpendicular format remove the old ones and apply new ones.
- **20.** There should be only 2 Tip 'N' Tell indicators on the perpendicular side of the crate.
  - ✓ Remove any additional Tip 'N' Tell indicators.
- **21.** If there are multiple indicators on the crate and only 2 are activated no visual inspection is needed, remove the additional indicators, ensure they are in a perpendicular format, and pull the pin holders.
- **22.** For any damage that is not resolvable on-site send pictures of damages to contacts listed below

**Mike Alaimo** – Senior Mgr. Fetch Operations, **Shailesh Jaiswal** – Manufacturing Engineer, **Eloy Rodas** – Quality Engineer, **Elba Maya** – SC Planner, and the onsite QA Team.

- **23.** Move the material to MRB1 and await disposition from the Team listed above.
- **24.** Place the checklist in the white pouch affixed to the crate's front.

### 3. PRE-CHARGING

- **1.** Pull the Inspection Checklist Rev01 from the pouch that is affixed to the crate.
- **2.** Add the date that corresponds with the charging time instance.
- **3.** Enter your initials in the Accepted or Rejected Columns when completing sections 2.1 2.7 of the Inspection Checklist.
  - ✓ Initials will be needed every time we are charging the Robots.
  - ✓ If we are charging the Robot more than 4 times a year, use another Checklist and staple it to the original.
- **4.** For rejections that are not resolvable on site contact the individuals listed in Section 2.22.

#### **Resolvable on-site:**

- Cleaning fingerprints, wheels, dust, or particles and/or replacing the desiccant bags and/or the cover bag.
- **5.** If Robot is not charged the same day, it was plugged in to be charged leave for overnight charging.
- **6.** Ensure the charging status is verified if the Robot was left for overnight charging.

## 4. POST-CHARGING

- **1.** Pull the Inspection Checklist Rev01 from the pouch that is affixed to the crate.
- **2.** Enter your initials in the Accepted or Rejected Columns when completing sections 3.1 3.7 of the Inspection Checklist.
- **3.** For rejections that are not resolvable on site contact the individuals listed in Section 2.22.

#### **Resolvable on-site:**

- Cleaning fingerprints, wheels, dust, or particles and/or replacing the desiccant bags and/or the cover bag.
- **4.** If need to clean the Robot with a Microfiber cloth to remove any fingerprints, dust, or particles.
- **5.** When Robot is ready to be put away, contact an Inbound associate, and put a green cone on the crate.
  - ✓ Only contact the Inbound associate when the Robot is ready to be put away to prevent idle time.
