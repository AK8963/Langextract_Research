# 1. **OVERVIEW**

This work instruction provides instructions on how to count small TX5 parts using the Scale Counter.

# 2. **BALANCE THE SCALE**

**1.** Calibrate the scale by centering the scale bubble.

![](_page_0_Picture_5.jpeg)

**2.** Use the 2 black turning knobs in front of the scale to adjust.

![](_page_0_Picture_7.jpeg)

✓ Balanced Scale

![](_page_0_Picture_9.jpeg)

**3.** Click Zero to reset the Scale.

![](_page_0_Picture_11.jpeg)

# 3. **COUNTING PROCESS**

- **1.** Place counting bowl on the scale.
  - ✓ Do not put any items directly on scale, always use bowl!
  - ✓ Make sure the bowl does not touch the edges of the scale.

![](_page_1_Picture_5.jpeg)

![](_page_1_Picture_6.jpeg)

- **2.** The scale will display the bowl weight, click Zero to reset.
- **3.** Click on Sample size until you reach one of the sample quantities below.

![](_page_1_Picture_9.jpeg)

- ✓ Bigger units sample size is 10
- ✓ Small units sample size is 25, 50 or 100
- ✓ Samples limit 25 units.

![](_page_1_Picture_13.jpeg)

![](_page_1_Picture_14.jpeg)

- ✓ Most of the parts can be counted using a sample size of 10. If the light next to "OK" doesn't light, a larger sample is needed.
- **4.** Press count when parts are placed, the scale will display the sample size weight. Ensure the light next to "OK" turns green. This indicates that the scale has successfully average the weight of the parts.

![](_page_2_Picture_3.jpeg)

**5.** Add the required number of parts to the bowl. The scale will capture the count of the parts.

![](_page_2_Picture_5.jpeg)

- ✓ Add a piece or two to make sure the quantity increased.
- ✓ Do not dump all the items at once, sprinkle around to try to even the items in the bowl.
- ✓ If there is a pile in the middle of the scale, with your finger even it out.
