# 1. OVERVIEW

The purpose of this work instruction is to provide the procedure for unpacking, charging, and repacking the RollerTop (RT)100 robot.

# 2. INSPECTION PROCESS

- **1.** Move the robot to the staging area—Reference *Subinventory Transfer Rev1.0 work instructions*.
- **2.** Inspect the shipment for any possible mishandling: (a) Crate, (b) Shockwatch indicator, and (c) Tip 'N' Tell indicator.
  - a) The crate should not have any writing or drawing on it.

![](_page_0_Picture_8.jpeg)

b) Shockwatch indicator of mishandling

![](_page_0_Picture_10.jpeg)

![](_page_0_Picture_11.jpeg)

Red indicates the shipment was mishandled.

c) Tip 'N' Tell indicator of mishandling.

![](_page_0_Picture_14.jpeg)

![](_page_0_Picture_15.jpeg)

If the arrow point is blue, the shipment was mishandled.

- **3.** Robots must be charged to full at least once every six months per the battery manufacturer's specifications.
  - ✓ If a unit is not changed at four or five and a half months, it must be charged at the 6-month mark regardless of the charge level.
- **4.** If the robot does not require charging, follow section 4 of this work instruction.

# 3. UNPACK PROCESS

- **1.** Move the freight crate to the charging area—Reference *Subinventory Transfer Rev1.0 work instruction*.
- **2.** Open it by using the latches on the top and sides.
- **3.** Remove the front cover.

![](_page_1_Picture_7.jpeg)

- **4.** Unsecure the strap across the ramp.
  - ✓ The strap could be yellow or black; there is no difference.

![](_page_1_Picture_10.jpeg)

- **5.** Fold down the ramp from the crate.
  - ✓ Do not step on the ramp.
  - ✓ The ramp should not have any markings.

![](_page_2_Picture_3.jpeg)

- **6.** Remove the Push/Slide wood-backed foam insert from the slots on the crate's sides.
  - ✓ If the foam detaches from each other, use the heat gun to warm up the glue and reattach the foam.

![](_page_2_Picture_6.jpeg)

Push/Slide Woodbacked Foam Insert.

![](_page_2_Picture_8.jpeg)

- **7.** Remove the clear tape from the barrier bag. If the bag is heat-sealed use scissors to cut the bag open.
  - ✓ If using scissors to cut the bag, try to cut it as smoothly as possible and as close to the sealing seam as possible to reuse the bag.
- **8.** Keep the desiccant bag inside the plastic bag in the crate.

![](_page_2_Picture_12.jpeg)

**9.** Use tape or clamps to secure the plastic bag to the ramp, side, and top of the wood panels so the bag is not in your way of unpacking the robot.

**10.** Once the Robot is accessible, remove the riser set between the roller top and the Freight100 base.

![](_page_3_Picture_1.jpeg)

- **11.** Store the Riser set in the bag inside the crate for the final pack out.
- **12.** Wear latex gloves and roll the RT100 out of the crate.
  - ✓ Do not touch the Camera and laser.

![](_page_3_Picture_5.jpeg)

- **13.** Inspect the robot for any visible damage.
- **14.** Keep all foam, the protective bag, the desiccant bag, and the riser set inside the crate and close the crate with the door with at least 2-3 latches.
- **15.** Reference *Manual Charging Process Rev03* to manually charge the robot battery.
- **16.** Ensure the Robot is cleaned.
- **17.** Robot Cleaning Guidelines:
  - a). Microfiber Lint-free towel/cloth will be used on a Class A area of the Robot (such as LCD screen & control panel).
  - b). IPA with shop towels or Kimwipes can be used if you find scuff marks on sheet metal.
  - c). Kimwipes can be used if you find fingerprints/marks on the screen, camera, and laser component.

**18.** Ensure the breaker switch is on the OFF position before repacking.

![](_page_4_Picture_1.jpeg)

Breaker Switch

# 4. REPACK PROCESS

**1.** Open the Freight Crate using the sides, top, and bottom latches, and remove the front cover.

![](_page_4_Picture_5.jpeg)

- **2.** Fold out the ramp completely to allow the robot to be rolled into the crate.
- **3.** Unfold the protective bag and remove the foam, desiccant bags, and riser set for repacking.
- **4.** Tape or clamp the opening of the protective bag to the bottom of the ramp, sides, and the top of the wood panels for easy loading of the robot into the crate.

![](_page_4_Picture_9.jpeg)

✓ The plastic bag could be black or translucent blue (most common).

**5.** Place the riser set between the Roller top and the Freight100 base.

![](_page_5_Picture_1.jpeg)

![](_page_5_Picture_2.jpeg)

**6.** Engage the RunStop button on the robot and slowly roll the robot up the ramp into the crate.

![](_page_5_Picture_4.jpeg)

✓ Ensure the back of the robot where the breaker is located faces the back of the crate, and the front of the robot faces outward; the front has the laser.

![](_page_5_Picture_6.jpeg)

- **7.** Reuse the original desiccant bags and place them as they were when unpacking the Robot.
  - ✓ Usually, 2-3 desiccant bags inside the protective bag and one outside the bag in the crate

![](_page_6_Picture_0.jpeg)

**8.** Fold the barrier bag and cover the Robot completely with the bag.

![](_page_6_Picture_2.jpeg)

- **9.** Apply clear tape to the bag.
  - ✓ Don't heat-sealed the bag.
- **10.** Slide the Push/Slide the wood-backed foam insert into the slots on the sides of the crate. Ensure the insert is pressed firmly against the front of the robot.

![](_page_6_Picture_6.jpeg)

**11.** Fold up the ramp into the crate.

![](_page_7_Picture_1.jpeg)

**12.** Secure the strap across the ramp.

![](_page_7_Picture_3.jpeg)

**13.** Align the top edge of the crate's front cover with the top lip of the crate and latch it into place.

![](_page_7_Picture_5.jpeg)

**14.** Align the bottom edge and clamp. You might need a rubber mallet to force the front cover into place.

![](_page_7_Picture_7.jpeg)

*This document contains proprietary information which is the exclusive property of Zebra Technologies Corp.*

- **15.** Latch the front cover's top and sides to the crate.
- **16.** The robot must be monitored for recharging until it is shipped.
- **17.** Put a green cone on the crate, move it to the staging area, and ask an associate to put it away.
