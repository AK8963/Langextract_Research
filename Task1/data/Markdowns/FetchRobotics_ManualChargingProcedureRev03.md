#### 1. OVERVIEW

The purpose of this work instruction is to provide the process for charging the Fetch Robot battery.

The battery levels dictate if charging is required. Battery levels are checked at four months and then again at a month and a half; if the battery was not charged at five and a half months, it must be charged at six months per the battery manufacturer.

## 2. CHARGING THE ROBOT

✓ Do not touch the Camera & Laser & Cart Connect Pins.

![](_page_0_Picture_7.jpeg)

![](_page_0_Picture_8.jpeg)

**1**. Turn ON the breaker switch.

![](_page_0_Picture_10.jpeg)

Breaker Switch

**2.** Press the power button to turn ON the access panel, let it sit for 15 minutes, and capture the battery charging bars.

✓ Do not touch any other buttons (display port, USB ports, and Ethernet port) on the panel.

![](_page_1_Picture_1.jpeg)

**3.** If the battery icon is displaying (*3 or more bars*), do not charge; repack the robot and request to be put away.

![](_page_1_Picture_3.jpeg)

**4.** If the battery icon displays (2 bars or less), charge the robot back to full by following the instructions below.

![](_page_1_Picture_5.jpeg)

- ✓ Regardless of the battery level at five and a half months, the Robot must be charged.
- ✓ The battery manufacturer recommends charging the battery at least 1x every six months.
- **5.** Bring the manual battery charger with power cable assembly close to the Robot.
  - ✓ A charger below could work with any line voltage between 100V and 240V AC.

![](_page_1_Picture_10.jpeg)

**6.** Plug the supply connector from the manual charger into the front of the Robot.

![](_page_2_Picture_1.jpeg)

- **7.** When using the supply connector, ensure the FETCH LOGO faces up and Green Nested Connectors are matched; otherwise, the connector will not correctly mate, and we could risk damaging the connectors.
  - ✓ The connector needs roughly 24 lbs. of force to plug it in.

![](_page_2_Picture_4.jpeg)

Fetch Logo

![](_page_2_Picture_6.jpeg)

**8.** The Robot lightning bolt symbol on the Access Panel will reflect the battery status and the charging process.

![](_page_2_Picture_8.jpeg)

| Charging Color Indicator |   | Meaning                     |   |   |
|--------------------------|---|-----------------------------|---|---|
| Solid Green              |   | Robot is fully charged      |   | 4 |
| Blinking Green           |   | Robot is charging           |   |   |
| Solid Red                |   | Battery voltage low         | ħ | 1 |
| Blinking Red             | Т | Charging Error has occurred |   | 7 |

- **9.** The battery is fully charged When ALL 5 LED bars and charging indicators are solid green. Capture the charged date for that robot in the system.
  - ✓ Every single Robot needs to have its own charge date.
- **10.** Remove the charge connector; when disconnecting the supply from the robot, grab the plug and pull it out.
  - ✓ Never pull on the cable to pull out the plug.

*This document contains proprietary information which is the exclusive property of Zebra Technologies Corp.*

- ✓ Pull straight back when disconnecting the supply plug; do not pull out at an angle. The connector is not designed to support large sideways forces.
- ✓ Do not attempt to move the robot while plugged into the charger.

![](_page_3_Picture_2.jpeg)

- **11.** Turn off the access panel by holding the power button for a few seconds; the access panel will turn off.
- **12.** Ensure the breaker switch is OFF before repacking the Robot.
  - ✓ If the switch break is left on, it will drain the battery.
