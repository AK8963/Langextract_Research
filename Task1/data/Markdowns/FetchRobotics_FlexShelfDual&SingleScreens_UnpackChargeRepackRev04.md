FLEXSHELF DUAL/SINGLE SCREENS – UNPACK, CHARGE, AND REPACK

#### 1. OVERVIEW

The purpose of this work instruction is to provide the procedure for unpacking, charging, and repacking the FlexShelf Dual/Single Screens Robot.

### 2. INSPECTION PROCESS

- **1.** Move the robot to the staging area—Reference *Subinventory Transfer Rev1.0 work instructions*.
- **2.** Inspect the shipment for any possible mishandling: (a) rate, (b) Shockwatch indicator, and (c) Tip 'N' Tell indicator.
  - a) The crate should not have any damage or writing or drawing.

![](_page_0_Picture_12.jpeg)

b) Shockwatch indicator of mishandling

![](_page_0_Picture_14.jpeg)

![](_page_0_Picture_15.jpeg)

Red indicates the shipment

c) Tip 'N' Tell indicator of mishandling.

![](_page_0_Picture_18.jpeg)

![](_page_0_Picture_19.jpeg)

If the arrow point is blue, the shipment was mishandled.

*This document contains proprietary information which is the exclusive property of Zebra Technologies Corp.*

- ✓ If you find any issues or concerns of mishandling, please notify the Zebra Engineering and Quality department immediately.
- **3.** Robots must be charged to full at least once every six months per the battery manufacturer's specifications.
  - ✓ If a unit is not changed at four or five and half months, it must be charged at the 6-month mark regardless of the charge level.
- **4.** If the robot does not require charging, follow section 4 of this work instruction.

#### 3. UNPACK PROCESS

- **1.** Move the freight crate to the charging area. Reference *Subinventory Transfer Rev1.0 work instruction*.
- **2.** Carefully open the front door latches (8 total) and remove the door.

![](_page_1_Picture_7.jpeg)

![](_page_1_Picture_8.jpeg)

**3.** Carefully open the top lid latches (6 total) and remove the top lid.

![](_page_1_Picture_10.jpeg)

- **4.** Unsecure the strap across the ramp.
  - ✓ The strap could be yellow or black; there is no difference.

![](_page_2_Picture_2.jpeg)

- **5.** Fold down the ramp from the crate.
  - ✓ Do not step on the ramp.
  - ✓ The ramp should not have any markings.
- **6.** Remove the Push/Slide wood-backed foam insert from the slots on the crate's sides.
  - ✓ If the foam detaches from each other, use the heat gun to warm up the glue and reattach the foam.

![](_page_2_Picture_8.jpeg)

**7.** Remove the foam inserts protecting the Robot screens located on top of the Robot.

![](_page_2_Picture_10.jpeg)

![](_page_2_Picture_11.jpeg)

![](_page_2_Picture_12.jpeg)

*This document contains proprietary information which is the exclusive property of Zebra Technologies Corp.*

- **8.** Remove the clear tape from the protective bag. If the bag is heat-sealed use scissors to cut the bag open.
  - ✓ If using scissors to cut the bag, try to cut it as smoothly as possible and as close to the sealing seam as possible to reuse the bag.
- **9.** Keep the desiccant bag inside the protective bag in the crate.

![](_page_3_Picture_3.jpeg)

- **10.** Use tape or plastic clamps to secure the protective bag to the ramp sides of the wood panels so the bag is not in your way of unpacking the robot.
- **11.** Wear latex gloves and roll out the Robot from the bag and the crate.

![](_page_3_Picture_6.jpeg)

![](_page_3_Picture_7.jpeg)

- **12.** Ensure you are rolling the Robot from the shelves as shown below; do not touch the screen, sensor, or camera.
- **13.** Inspect the robot for any visible damage.
- **14.** Keep all foam, protective bag, and desiccant bags inside the crate and close the crate with the door with at least 2-3 latches.
- **15.** Reference *Manual Charging Process Rev03* to manually charge the robot battery.
- **16.** Ensure the Robot is cleaned.
- **17.** Robot Cleaning Guidelines:
  - a). Microfiber Lint-free towel/cloth will be used on a Class A area of the Robot (such as LCD screen & control panel).

- b). IPA with shop towels or Kimwipes can be used if you find scuff marks on sheet metal.
- c). Kimwipes can be used if you find fingerprints/marks on the screen, camera, and laser component.
- **18.** Ensure the breaker switch is on the OFF position before repacking.

![](_page_4_Picture_3.jpeg)

#### 4. REPACK PROCESS

- **1.** Bring the Robot close to the crate, open the crate latches, and remove the strap from the ramp.
  - ✓ Ensure the Velcro on the ramp and the bottom of the crate match when the ramp is laid down.

![](_page_4_Picture_7.jpeg)

![](_page_4_Picture_8.jpeg)

![](_page_4_Picture_9.jpeg)

**2.** Fold out the ramp completely to allow the robot to be rolled into the crate.

![](_page_4_Picture_11.jpeg)

*This document contains proprietary information which is the exclusive property of Zebra Technologies Corp.*

**3.** Remove the top cover of the crate by unleashing the locks on all 4-sides.

![](_page_5_Picture_1.jpeg)

- **4.** Unfold the protective bag and remove the foam and desiccant bags for repacking.
- **5.** Tape or clamp the opening of the protective bag to the bottom of the ramp and side wood panels for effortless loading of the robot into the crate.

![](_page_5_Picture_4.jpeg)

- **6.** Reuse the original desiccant bags and place them as they were when unpacking the Robot.
  - ✓ Usually, 2-3 desiccant bags are inside the protective bag, and one is outside the bag in the crate.

![](_page_5_Picture_7.jpeg)

- **7.** Engage the RunStop button on the robot. Place the robot near the ramp backward and roll the robot into the bag and the crate as shown. Make sure that the robot is resting in the crate all the way and touching the foam.
  - ✓ Ensure the back of the robot where the breaker is located faces the back of the crate, and the front of the robot faces outward; the front has the camera and laser.

![](_page_6_Picture_2.jpeg)

![](_page_6_Picture_3.jpeg)

![](_page_6_Picture_4.jpeg)

![](_page_6_Picture_5.jpeg)

- **8.** Fold the barrier bag and cover the robot completely.
- **9.** Apply clear tape to the bag to close it.
  - ✓ Don't heat-sealed the bag.

![](_page_6_Picture_9.jpeg)

**10.** Install the left half of the top foam on the robot's top, as shown. The top of the foam should be flush with the top edge of the crate. Ensure that both flex screen(s) are correctly seated inside the cutout of the foam. In the case of Dual screen, repeat the same process for the right side of the foam.

![](_page_7_Picture_1.jpeg)

![](_page_7_Picture_2.jpeg)

- **11.** Push/Slide the wood-backed foam insert into the slots on the sides of the crate and ensure the top foam insert is pressed firmly against the Robot.
- **12.** Fold up the ramp into the crate and latch it.

![](_page_7_Picture_5.jpeg)

✓ Ensure the strap is tight enough to hold the ramp in place.

![](_page_8_Picture_0.jpeg)

**13.** Place the top lid back on the crate and latch all four sides of the lid with the crate. This may require a rubber/plastic mallet to latch on all four sides—a total of 6 latches.

![](_page_8_Picture_2.jpeg)

**14.** Place the main door back into place and secure it with the latches (8 total)

![](_page_8_Picture_4.jpeg)

- **15.** The robot must be monitored for recharging until it is shipped.
- **16.** Put a green cone on the crate, move it to the staging area, and ask an associate to put it away.
