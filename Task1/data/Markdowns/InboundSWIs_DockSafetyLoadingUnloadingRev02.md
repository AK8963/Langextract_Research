# 1. OVERVIEW

The purpose of this work instruction is to provide a step-by-step guide to assist in developing site-specific dock safety controls. The intention is to ensure flawless safety in preventing injuries to employees, customers, vendors, and contingent workers.

# 3. DRIVER ARRIVAL

- **1.** The DRIVER reports to the gate entrance upon arrival at the warehouse.
- **2.** The DRIVER reviews Yard Driver Safety Rules if any are available.
  - ✓ All drivers must review this form before entering the facility yard for the first time. At this time, the Security representative will answer any questions the driver may have. If there are any issues, please notify Management for additional help.
  - ✓ Zebra representative ensures drivers understand and acknowledge the safety procedures.

**3.** Security radios respective department for door assignment and/or further instructions.

# 4. SECURING THE TRAILER AT THE DOCK

- **1.** To ensure the safety of the employees while loading and unloading trailers, a Zebra associate will assign the trailer & dock door to be worked on.
- **2.** The driver backs up the trailer to the assigned door.
  - ✓ If doors have a seal, the Zebra associate must break the seal.
- **3.** If the vehicle has no roll-up door, the driver opens the swing doors in front of the assigned door. Ensure swing doors are secured to the side of the trailer to avoid accidental movement, which could damage the dock door or building.
- **4.** Ensure that a minimum of 2 primary controls secure the vehicle/trailer to prevent the possibility of unintentional departure of the trailer.
  - ✓ Use **Section 2** of this document to identify the controls for your loading/unloading operation.
  - ✓ If any controls are missing, damaged, inoperable, or ineffective, STOP the process and notify management.
- **5.** Ensure the door handle and/or strapping are secured and in good condition when opening or closing the dock bay door.
- **6.** All dock doors (inbound and outbound) must be closed.
- **7.** Security latch must be in the closed position (when not in use).
- **8.** The Dock associate must visually verify two-wheel chocks are in place; if not, do not proceed until wheel chocks are in place before moving to the Unloading or Loading Process.

# 5. UNLOADING PROCESS (INBOUND)

- **1.** The dock associate opens the dock overhead bay door.
- **2.** The Zebra associate must inspect the inside of the trailer using [Zebra Trailer Inspection and Release Form](https://zebra.sharepoint.com/:b:/r/sites/KenoshaDistributionCenter/Shared%20Documents/EHS/Zebra%20Trailer%20Inspection%20and%20Release%20Form%20Rev01xls.pdf?csf=1&web=1&e=QVGEGT)  [Rev01xls.pdf](https://zebra.sharepoint.com/:b:/r/sites/KenoshaDistributionCenter/Shared%20Documents/EHS/Zebra%20Trailer%20Inspection%20and%20Release%20Form%20Rev01xls.pdf?csf=1&web=1&e=QVGEGT)
- **3.** If the inspection identifies any issues, close the dock door and notify your supervisor for resolution.
- **4.** If Dock Lights are available, use them to provide more visibility inside the trailer.
- **5.** The unloader extends the dock plate until it is fully extended.
  - ✓ The lip of the dock plate must be in complete contact with the inside of the trailer.
  - ✓ DO NOT DRIVE a Power Industrial Vehicle (PIV) ONTO THE BED OF ANY BOX/STRAIGHT TRUCK.
  - ✓ Utilize manual pallet jack for all unloading of Box / Straight Truck.
- **6.** Stop the PIV each time you exit the trailer; activate the horn and be aware of other PIVs or pedestrians.
- **7.** Zebra Personnel must remain outside the lines of the bay and the trailer during unloading.
- **8.** During the unloading process, the Unloader must verify that the inventory and dunnage are safe to handle.

- **9.** Dunnage used on the trailer should be disposed of properly.
- **10.** The product removed from the trailer should be staged in a specified zone with cones separating the load from others working in a nearby door.
  - ✓ While staging, there should be enough room between pallets for case & pallet breakdown and repalletizing.

# 6. LOADING PROCESS (OUTBOUND)

- **1.** The dock associate opens the dock overhead bay door.
- **2.** The Zebra associate must inspect the inside of the trailer using [Zebra Trailer Inspection and Release Form](https://zebra.sharepoint.com/:b:/r/sites/KenoshaDistributionCenter/Shared%20Documents/EHS/Zebra%20Trailer%20Inspection%20and%20Release%20Form%20Rev01xls.pdf?csf=1&web=1&e=QVGEGT)  [Rev01xls.pdf](https://zebra.sharepoint.com/:b:/r/sites/KenoshaDistributionCenter/Shared%20Documents/EHS/Zebra%20Trailer%20Inspection%20and%20Release%20Form%20Rev01xls.pdf?csf=1&web=1&e=QVGEGT)
- **3.** If the inspection identifies any issues, close the dock door and notify your supervisor for resolution.
- **4.** If Dock Lights are available, use them to provide more visibility inside the trailer.
- **5.** The loader extends the dock plate until it is fully extended.
  - ✓ The lip of the dock plate must be in complete contact with the inside of the trailer.
- **6.** The loader identifies the material to be loaded according to the Bill of Lading (BOL).
- **7.** Identify the trailer as a Box Truck/Straight Truck
  - ✓ DO NOT DRIVE PIV ONTO THE BED OF ANY BOX/STRAIGHT TRUCK.
  - ✓ Utilize manual pallet jack for all loading of Box/Straight Truck.
- **8.** Stop the PIV each time you exit the trailer; activate the horn and be aware of other PIVs or pedestrians.
- **9.** Zebra Personnel must remain outside the bay and trailer lines during loading.
- **10.** During the loading process, the material handlers must verify that the freight is appropriately secure and free of damage before loading.

# 7. RELEASE THE DRIVER

- **1.** Exit the trailer and remove any PIVs (from the dock loading area), tools, equipment, etc.
- **2.** Loader / Unloader removes or disengages the dock plate.
- **3.** The loader/unloader closes and locks the overhead bay door.
- **4.** Loader removes Primary Controls according to the Matrix in Section 2 of this document (i.e., Dock lock, then glad hand, etc.).
- **5.** Loader applies trailer seal if applicable (outbound loads only).
- **6.** A Zebra representative provides a signed copy of the BOL and any other necessary documents to the driver.
- **7.** The truck driver removes Wheel Chocks and leaves the dock door.
