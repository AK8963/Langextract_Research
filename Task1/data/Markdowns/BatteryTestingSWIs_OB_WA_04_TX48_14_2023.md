## **1. PURPOSE**

1.1. To define a battery management process to restrict the shipment from TX4/5 to customers and partners, of rechargeable battery packs that do not meet Zebra's battery quality standards and requirements. This procedure will outline the process to periodically purge battery stock from inventory and perform testing to ensure product in stock is meeting quality standards prior to shipping.

#### **2. SCOPE**

- 2.1. This procedure is applicable to the TX4/5 distribution center and defines the minimum requirements for managing battery inventory.
- 2.2. Applies to both EVM and ATS batteries.
- 2.3. Applies to batteries received from battery pack suppliers.
- 2.4. Applies to batteries returned through the Stock Rotation process (re-stockable items).
- 2.5. Limited to lithium-ion batteries, this excludes Lithium metal, nickel metal, lead acid batteries and coin cells.
- 2.6. Not applicable to batteries packaged with product.

#### **4. DEFINITIONS / ABBREVIATIONS / ACRONYMS**

- 4.1. DC: Distribution Center
- 4.2. ATS: Asset Identification and Tracking including printers, printing supplies and location solutions.
- 4.3. EVM: Enterprise Visibility and Mobility including mobile computers, and scanning solutions
- 4.4. Stock Rotation: Product returned from distributors and deemed acceptable for restock
- 4.5. Shelf Life: Defined length of time that batteries are considered acceptable to ship
- 4.6. Date Code: Defines the Manufacture date of the battery pack on the overpack (a package of individual packages of batteries) not the individual battery pack. Date codes are in various formats (see Appendix A)
- 4.7. MRB: Material Review Board (Cross functional team that determines disposition of suspect material)
- 4.8. RMA: Return Material Authorization
- 4.9. Battery Lot: A group of batteries with the same inventory part number, and battery pack label part number manufactured on the same day by the same manufacturer.
- 4.10. Inter DC shipments: Shipments between DC's for example a shipment from NL1 to TX4.
- 4.11. WIP Job: Work In Progress Job
- 4.12. Sub-inventory REW2: TX4/5's Rework locator, non-standard WIP Job are always released from REW2.
- 4.13. Sub-inventory RMF1: Re-Configuration sub-inventory, WIP Jobs need to be dropped to this sub-inventory to start the actual job.
- 4.14. Sub-inventory FIG1: Finished goods sub-inventory.
- 4.15. OAC: Oracle Analytics Cloud is a platform to access and process data.
- 4.16. OPS: Operations
- 4.17. IC: Inventory Control

  - 5.1.3. **Testing Equipment:** Test Engineer (Jesse Abruzzo)

- 5.12. **Operations:** Execute battery lot acceptance test according to OB-TE-01.
- 5.13. **Operations:** Return to stock in original packaging those lots that are deemed acceptable based on testing criteria; exclusive of those batteries which were capacity-tested – these should be scrapped

## **6. PROCESS**

#### 6.1. **Incoming (Receiving)**

- 6.1.1. Receive in batteries from supplier according to work instructions.
- 6.1.2. Receive in internal shipments according to work instructions.
  - 6.1.2.1. E-mail quality to get rid of inspection flag.
  - 6.1.2.2. Follow quality's advice (either move to FIG1/FWP1 or REW2).

#### 6.2. **DC Incoming (Return Material Authorization – Stock Rotations)**

6.2.1. Battery Lots returned from Zebra partners must be checked for manufacturing date before being placed in inventory. This date must be used to determine if the Lot is subject to testing.

#### 6.3. **Put Away**

- 6.3.1. Supplier batteries move to FIG1/FWP1
- 6.3.2. Internal & Stock Rotation batteries move to FIG1/FWP1 if younger than 1 year or move to REW2 if older than 1 year.

### 6.4. **Monthly Report**

- 6.4.1. Quality will run a monthly report from OAC.
- 6.4.2. Quality will determine impacted (according to OB-WA-04) battery lots.
- 6.4.3. Inventory Control will move impacted battery lots that Quality determined to REW2.
- 6.4.4. Quality will communicate battery testing results & age with planning, OPS & IC on an as needed basis through MRB meeting, NCR meeting, and/or through email.

#### 6.5. **Raise WIP Job**

6.5.1. Planner will raise WIP Job with pick release for battery testing.

#### 6.6. **Pick Material**

6.6.1. Picker will pick material and physical drop in Re-Configuration Staging area outside the fence.

## 6.7. **Drop Material & Run Test**

- 6.7.1. Re-Configuration operator will drop material in system to RMF1.
- 6.7.2. Re-Configuration operator will perform test according to OB-TE-01.
- 6.7.3. Test results will be automatically recorded in Splunk.

#### 6.8. **Scrap Tested Batteries**

6.8.1. Re-Configuration operator with Supervisor/Manager will scrap batteries according to waste management.
